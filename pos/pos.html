Pilot of Singularity Game

1. Game goal

You are piloting a gravitational singularity (also known as Black Hole) through a crowded universe. Absorb small bodies to grow your mass and survive as long as possible while Hawking radiation constantly reduces your mass.

The game ends if your mass reaches zero.

2. Controls

  • Arrow keys: apply thrust in the selected direction.
  • Mouse drag: drag from the singularity to set thrust direction and intensity.
  • Touch drag: same behavior as mouse drag for mobile and touch devices.
  • Exit button (×): stop the game and return to this start page.

3. Heads-up information

The top-right corner shows your current mass, maximum mass reached, speed, elapsed play time, and estimated remaining lifetime.

4. Strategy tips

  • Use gentle, controlled thrust bursts to avoid wasting too much mass.
  • Prioritize dense clusters of small bodies for faster growth.
  • Keep an eye on speed and remaining lifetime to balance aggression and survival.

5. Physical effects simulated

  • Paczyński–Wiita pseudo-Newtonian gravitational potential to approximate general-relativistic gravitational singularity gravity (approximate simulation).
  • Relativistic velocity composition for thrust updates with the speed of light as a hard limit (exact relativistic formula used in the program flow).
  • Mass-energy tradeoff during thrust where accelerating costs mass (approximate simulation).
  • Hawking radiation mass loss over time using the Hawking evaporation relation for a Schwarzschild gravitational singularity (exact equation used by the program model).
  • Absorption and growth when nearby small bodies are captured by the singularity and their mass is added to the singularity (exact rule in the game model).
  • GS halo temperature/color behavior represented by mass-driven halo coloring (approximate visualization).
  • Gravitational lensing represented the light bending effect by strong Gravitational Singularity gravity (Newtonian Weak-Field Gravity approximation).
  • Toroidal universe wrapping where objects leaving one boundary re-enter from the opposite side (gameplay approximation).

6. Effects currently excluded

  • Time dilation.
  • Frame dragging.
  • Doppler shift.
